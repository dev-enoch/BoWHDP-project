1. Executive Summary
The Borno Works & Housing Delivery Platform (BoWHDP) is an integrated, web and mobile-based Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E) system designed to give the Honourable Commissioner of Works & Housing complete, real-time visibility over every road, housing, bridge, and public works project in Borno State.
The platform consolidates eight specialised modules — geo-tracking, field verification, contractor scorecards, citizen feedback, drone-based progress verification, predictive risk analytics, budget-versus-delivery tracking, and automated quarterly public reports — into a single, unified delivery intelligence system.
BoWHDP is aligned with the Borno State 25-Year Development Framework and His Excellency Governor Prof. Babagana Umara Zulum's commitment to transparency, value-for-money, and citizen-centred governance. The platform is designed for full deployment within 12 months, with the first visible wins delivered inside the Commissioner's first 100 days in office.
1.1 Document Purpose
This PRD defines the business need, scope, functional and non-functional requirements, technical architecture, delivery roadmap, governance model, and acceptance criteria for the BoWHDP platform. It serves as the binding reference document between the Ministry of Works & Housing (the Client) and Haigha Technology (the Implementing Partner).
1.2 Target Outcomes
Eliminate ghost projects through geo-tagged, evidence-based field verification.
Reduce project delivery slippage by at least 30% within the first 12 months.
Recover overpayment leakage through drone and milestone-based payment verification.
Increase citizen trust through transparent, LGA-level public reporting.
Provide the Commissioner with weekly, executive-level delivery intelligence for decision-making.

2. Project Background & Context
2.1 The Current Situation
The Ministry of Works & Housing manages a large, geographically dispersed portfolio of road, housing, bridge, and public building projects across Borno State's 27 Local Government Areas. Project monitoring is currently characterised by:
Paper-based progress reporting with limited or no real-time visibility.
Manual, often delayed site visits — particularly difficult in security-affected LGAs.
Limited integration between budget disbursement and verified physical delivery.
Reactive rather than predictive identification of failing or at-risk projects.
Weak citizen feedback channels, especially for non-smartphone users in rural areas.
2.2 The Strategic Opportunity
With a newly appointed Commissioner, the Ministry has a clear window to institute a modern, technology-driven M&E culture. Borno State has already demonstrated leadership in digital governance through initiatives such as BOGIS (land portal), the BOSETF analytical dashboard, and the BOSCHMA contributory health scheme platform — all delivered locally by Haigha Technology.
BoWHDP extends this digital governance trajectory into the works and housing sector, where the economic stakes — and reputational stakes for His Excellency's administration — are highest.
2.3 Alignment with State Priorities
The platform is explicitly aligned with:
The Borno State 25-Year Development Framework (infrastructure and digital governance pillars).
His Excellency's 10-Point Agenda on transparency and service delivery.
National Digital Economy Policy and Strategy (NDEPS) 2020–2030.
Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 9, 11, and 16.

3. Project Scope
3.1 In Scope
The BoWHDP project includes the design, build, deployment, training, and post-launch support of the following:
A central web-based delivery dashboard for the Commissioner and senior ministry officials.
A field verification mobile application (Android-first, offline-capable) for engineers and monitors.
A contractor performance scorecard module integrated with procurement workflows.
A citizen feedback channel via USSD short code and WhatsApp business API.
A drone verification protocol and reporting workflow for high-value projects.
A predictive risk analytics engine for early identification of failing projects.
A budget-versus-delivery reconciliation tracker integrated with Ministry of Finance data.
An automated quarterly public delivery report generator and citizen-facing webpage.
Training of ministry staff (technical and non-technical), engineers, and selected contractors.
12 months of post-launch maintenance, hosting, and Service Level Agreement (SLA) support.
3.2 Out of Scope
Procurement of contractor-side hardware (vehicles, machinery, etc.).
Direct project execution, supervision, or contract management.
Internal Ministry of Finance accounting system replacement.
Hardware procurement for end-users beyond the agreed device allocation list.
3.3 Assumptions
The Ministry will appoint a dedicated Project Sponsor and a Project Steering Committee.
The Ministry will grant access to existing project records, budget data, and contractor registries.
Internet connectivity, however intermittent, will be available at major ministry locations.
A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) will be signed with the Ministry of Finance for budget data integration.
3.4 Constraints
Security limitations may restrict physical access to certain LGAs (Bama, Gwoza, Marte, Abadam, Mobbar).
Connectivity in remote LGAs requires offline-first architecture.
Drone deployment is subject to NCAA, NAF, and state security clearance.
Initial budget envelope and timeline as agreed in the signed Service Agreement.

7.1 Architectural Approach
BoWHDP follows a modular, API-first architecture built on proven open-source technologies. Each of the eight modules is a logical sub-system that communicates with a central data layer through well-defined APIs, enabling phased rollout, independent updates, and future integrations.

7.3 System Architecture Diagram (Logical)
The platform follows a four-layer logical architecture:
Presentation Layer — Web dashboard, mobile app, USSD menus, WhatsApp bot, public report site.
API Gateway Layer — Authentication, rate limiting, API routing to micro-services.
Service Layer — Eight modular services (one per module) plus shared services (auth, notifications, reporting).
Data Layer — PostgreSQL primary store, PostGIS for spatial data, object storage for imagery, data warehouse for analytics.
7.4 Integration Points
Ministry of Finance — Budget release and disbursement data (API or scheduled file exchange).
BOGIS — Land and parcel data for housing projects.
BOSETF Dashboard — Cross-ministry KPI alignment.
Contractor Registry (BPP) — Bidder validation.
Drone Service Provider — Imagery upload via secure API.
Telco / USSD Aggregator — Citizen feedback channel.

10. Project Timeline & Roadmap
The project will be delivered in three phases over 12 months. Each phase ends with a concrete, demonstrable milestone.
10.1 Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 0–3)
Objective: Give the Commissioner a working delivery dashboard within his first 100 days.
Discovery, requirements sign-off, system design.
Module 1 — Geo-Tracking Dashboard (production).
Module 2 — Field Verification App (production).
Module 3 — Contractor Scorecard (production).
Migration of existing project portfolio data.
Training of executive users and first cohort of engineers.
10.2 Phase 2 — Transparency (Months 4–6)
Objective: Open the system to citizens and connect to budget data.
Module 4 — Citizen Feedback (USSD + WhatsApp) live.
Module 7 — Budget vs Delivery Tracker live.
Module 8 — Quarterly Public Report (first edition published).
Public launch event with His Excellency the Governor.
10.3 Phase 3 — Intelligence (Months 7–12)
Objective: Add predictive and aerial-verification intelligence.
Module 5 — Drone Verification operational on first 10 high-value projects.
Module 6 — Predictive Risk Engine live with weekly Commissioner alerts.
Full integration of all modules; advanced analytics layer.
Knowledge transfer to ministry IT staff; transition to long-term SLA.

11. Governance & Project Management
11.1 Governance Structure
Project Sponsor — Honourable Commissioner of Works & Housing.
Steering Committee — Chaired by the Permanent Secretary; includes Directors, MoF representative, and Haigha leadership.
Project Management Office (PMO) — Joint team led by Haigha PM with Ministry counterpart.
Working Groups — Module-specific working groups for requirements clarification and UAT.
11.2 Reporting Cadence
Weekly — PMO status updates to Steering Committee chair.
Bi-weekly — Steering Committee meeting.
Monthly — Executive briefing to the Honourable Commissioner.
Quarterly — Strategic review with His Excellency's office.
11.3 Change Management
All changes to scope, requirements, or timeline must be raised via a formal Change Request, evaluated by the PMO, and approved by the Steering Committee. Changes with budget implications require Commissioner approval.
11.4 Methodology
The project will follow an Agile-Hybrid methodology — Waterfall for top-level phase gates and procurement discipline, Scrum within each phase for two-week development sprints with continuous demos to the Ministry.
